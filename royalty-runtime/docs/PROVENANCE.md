# Historical Provenance Analysis: Healing, Attribution, and the Politics of Canonization

**A cross-disciplinary investigation sparked by the open source compensation debate**

**March 2026**

---

## Context

During the debate that produced the Royalty Runtime, the conversation detoured into historical provenance — the Essene community, early Christian healing, Dead Sea Scrolls, and canonization politics. The parallels to open source attribution are structural: knowledge created communally, attributed selectively, monetized by institutions that didn't create it.

## 1. The Essene-Jesus Connection

The Essene community at Qumran practiced communal living, ritual purification, and healing. Key parallels with early Christianity: communal property (Josephus, Jewish War 2.122; Acts 2:44-45), ritual immersion, healing practices (the name Essene may derive from Aramaic asaya/healers), eschatological worldview. However, direct evidence that Jesus was an Essene does not exist. Scholars like VanderKam, Schiffman, and Vermes note shared Second Temple Jewish milieu rather than direct derivation. Bart Ehrman (alive and teaching at UNC Chapel Hill) has been clear on this distinction.

## 2. Ancient Healing and Oil-Based Treatments

Oil-based healing was pervasive across ancient cultures — Egyptian papyri, Mesopotamian medicine, Greek/Roman practice, Jewish tradition. The Talmudic references to oil-based treatments are medical texts reflecting practical Greco-Roman knowledge adapted into Jewish legal discourse, not mystical texts. The critical point: these healing traditions were widely shared. Early Christianity attributed common medical knowledge to divine power. The attribution was contested, not the method.

## 3. The Politics of Canonization

The biblical canon emerged through centuries of political process. Criteria included apostolic authority (often retroactive), orthodoxy (aligned with winning factions), widespread use (conservative bias), and political alignment (Constantine's unification project). Excluded texts: Gospel of Thomas, Gospel of Mary, Didache, 1 Enoch. Celsus (2nd century philosopher, unrelated to Celsius the temperature scale) critiqued Christianity in The True Word, preserved only in Origen's rebuttal.

## 4. The Provenance Parallel

| Ancient Pattern | Open Source Pattern |
|----------------|-------------------|
| Healing knowledge shared across cultures | Code shared across the ecosystem |
| Attributed to divine power by institutions | Attributed to corporate innovation by companies |
| Original practitioners received no credit | Original maintainers receive no compensation |
| Canon determined by political power | Standards determined by corporate adoption |
| Excluded texts preserved in fragments | Excluded contributors preserved in git blame |

The Royalty Runtime is an attempt to build the provenance system that history failed to create for ancient knowledge traditions.
