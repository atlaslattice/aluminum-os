## THESIS
Neuromorphic computing mimics the brain's architecture: neurons that spike, synapses that learn, and massively parallel processing where memory and computation are co-located. This paradigm achieves 100-1000x energy efficiency over conventional processors for suitable workloads - potentially solving AI's sustainability crisis.

## NEUROMORPHIC ADVANTAGES
1. **Event-driven**: Compute only on spikes, not continuously
2. **Co-located memory**: No von Neumann bottleneck
3. **Sparse activation**: Most neurons silent most of the time
4. **Temporal coding**: Information in spike timing
5. **Online learning**: Continuous adaptation without retraining
