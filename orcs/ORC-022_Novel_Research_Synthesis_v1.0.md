## §6 New Proposed Doctrines (D-95/D-96/D-97)

### D-95: Regenerative Compute Obligation

> Every inference routed through ORC infrastructure SHOULD prefer renewable-powered nodes when available at equivalent latency (±50ms). Carbon savings MUST be measurable, verifiable, and reported in TransparencyPacket `metabolic.renewable_percentage`. Credits accrued MUST belong to the user, not the platform.

**Rationale:** Makes "Regenerative" in ORC's name a literal, measurable commitment rather than an aspiration. Creates self-funding mechanism through tokenized environmental impact.

### D-96: Standards-Track Identity Preference

> When multiple identity mechanisms achieve equivalent security and user experience, ORC MUST prefer the standards-track option (W3C, IETF, FIDO Alliance) over proprietary alternatives. Proprietary mechanisms are acceptable only when no standards-track equivalent exists or when the standard is demonstrably inferior in security.

**Rationale:** Ensures long-term interoperability and prevents vendor lock-in at the identity layer. Standards outlive companies.

### D-97: Hardware Root Universality

> The credential vault (M125) MUST support hardware-backed key storage on every major platform (Apple Secure Enclave, Android StrongBox, Windows Hello TPM, ChromeOS Titan-C, Linux FIDO2) through a single protocol (WebAuthn/FIDO2). No platform may be excluded from hardware-root security. Graceful degradation to software-only storage is permitted with reduced token lifetime and mandatory biometric re-verification.

**Rationale:** Hardware trust should not be a privilege of expensive devices. FIDO2/WebAuthn provides the universal protocol that makes this achievable.
