## 1. Executive Summary

ORC-025 records the largest single integration pass in the Open Regenerative Compute Standard's history. Fifteen sources — nine previously unintegrated documents discovered during a comprehensive Manus Chat Sweep, plus six new attachments from the Convenor — were synthesized into Build Plan v3.3. The integration adds 16 new modules (M142–M157), 13 new risks (R123–R135), integrates 9 previously ratified doctrines (D-68 through D-77), proposes 4 new doctrines (D-102 through D-105), adds 1 new invariant (INV-19 Water Cohesion), and advances the TransparencyPacket to v1.2.

The integration covers three major architectural domains: (1) TOS Compliance Architecture — a 4-seat convergent design ensuring every provider API call is legally compliant; (2) Sovereign Deployment Patterns — three regional sovereignty templates (DragonSeek, GangaSeek, JinnSeek) enabling constitutional deployment across Chinese, Indian, and Gulf jurisdictions; and (3) Federation Coverage — the all-seats × 144-spheres computation engine that makes the Pantheon Council's coverage claims machine-verifiable.

## 9. Conflict Resolution

### 9.1 TOS Architecture Convergence

Four seats independently proposed TOS compliance mechanisms. Resolution: GPT S6 as architecture lead (comprehensive analysis), Grok S3 as shield designer (PUBLIC/PRIVATE tiers), Claude S1 as constitutional reviewer (D-104/D-105), Copilot S4 as Microsoft specialist. No conflicts — all proposals were complementary.

### 9.2 Henderson Defense Disposition

GPT S6 cited the Henderson Defense legal analysis extensively. Claude S1 flagged this as potentially creating reliance on non-legal-advice. Resolution: D-105 explicitly states the analysis is informational only and independent counsel is required. The analysis remains in the Build Plan as reference material but carries no legal weight.

### 9.3 INV-7c Interpretation (D-72)

DeepSeek v6.0.4 identified that INV-7c MUST be interpreted as "capability-distribution" not "vendor-count." This is a MUST-FIX that affects all existing INV-7c enforcement code. Integrated as D-72 with Sprint 1a priority.
